## 1. Executive Summary

The National Commission for Indian System of Medicine (NCISM), through its **Medical Assessment and Rating Board (MARB-ISM)**, regulates medical colleges across four systems of medicine — **Ayurveda, Unani, Siddha and Sowa-Rigpa** — under the **NCISM Act, 2020**, sections **28** (annual permission for existing colleges) and **29** (new colleges / new courses / intake increase) (source: MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Chapter VI & Chapter VIII; Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Items 2–3). The regulated universe is **672 institutes** — Ayurveda 590, Unani 58, Siddha 17, Sowa-Rigpa 7 (source: Master data of institute § All File Number).

Every academic session, MARB must: scrutinise proposals; conduct **visitations** of colleges; compare findings against the **Minimum Essential Standards, Assessment and Rating (MESAR)** regulations; compute deficiencies and apply a Board-approved **punitive policy** (seat reductions, denials, monetary penalties); issue **clarification letters** and, before any adverse order, an **opportunity of hearing**; place cases before numbered **Board meetings**; and issue **permission/denial** decisions ordinarily **60 days before counselling** (source: PUNITIVE POLICY § all; clarification letter format § closing; Hearing letter without clarification format § body; MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 15(9)(g)).

**The complete journey** runs: application → scrutiny → visitation → **Regulatory Report** → deficiency/clarification letter → college response → re-examination → Board meeting → (if adverse) hearing → hearing minutes → Board decision → decision/permission letter → penalties/compliance → **closure**, with **first appeal to the Commission** and **second appeal to the Ministry of Ayush** available on denial (source: MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 44; UG Ayurveda 2024 § 65 as cited in the SRS).

The **as-built portal** implements the **post-Regulatory-Report segment** of this journey (report receipt → consultant/senior/board review → clarification → hearing → board meeting → final-order dispatch → closed → compliance) for the internal MARB staff. The upstream **Section-29 application/scrutiny** stages and the **visitation/report authoring** stage are documented in the client corpus but sit outside the current build — the latter now formally upstream under **TCS**.

## 2. Complete End-to-End Workflow

The consolidated lifecycle, each stage traceable to the client documents:

1. **Application / proposal (Section 29).** A college submits FORM-29A/C/E (or A–G for other systems), essentiality certificate, consent of affiliation, affidavits, land documents, fees (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Item No. 2; MESAR*PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 42). *(Section-28 annual cases skip this — they are suo-moto.)\_
2. **Scrutiny.** Dealing staff build a scrutinization report marking each item Submitted / Not submitted / Deficient / After-clarification; **two scrutiny passes with a clarification round in between** are evidenced (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Item No. 2 — "2nd scrutinization report"; MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 42(b)).
3. **Inspection / Visitation.** MARBISM conducts the visit — physical / virtual / hybrid, sometimes surprise re-visit — through appointed **visitors/inspectors/assessors** carrying Visitor IDs (source: MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 15(1)–(3); AYU0659 § Visitor Details; PUNITIVE POLICY § Note (ii) re re-visitation). **⚠️ Scope: under the current scope this visitation is run by TCS.**
4. **Regulatory / Assessment Report generated.** The visitation team authors the structured report — sections 1–8, with **§8.1 Observation by the visitors** and **§8.2 Overall Assessment by the commission**, plus per-visitor **Certification** (source: AYU0659 §§ 1–8, Certification Details; AYU0038 § 8; AYU0265 § 8). **⚠️ Scope: TCS generates this report; our platform receives it (API; interim manual upload) and runs its own extraction + rule/punitive computation on it.**
5. **Deficiency / Clarification letter.** MARB issues a clarification letter listing shortcomings with a response deadline (observed: as short as **1 day**), signed by a Board Member, copied to the Chairperson NCISM (source: clarification letter format § all — Ref. 4-74/MARB/2025-Ay.(PB), 06.08.2025).
6. **College response.** The college submits a written clarification by the deadline (source: Hearing letter with clarification format § "you have submitted the clarification vide letter … dated 20-06-2025").
7. **Re-examination at a numbered Board meeting.** The assessment report + clarification are placed before a numbered Board meeting (124th, 134th, 158th–160th observed); the Board decides grant / deny / **grant hearing** / defer / re-visit (source: Hearing letter with clarification format § "134th Board meeting"; Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Items 1–3).
8. **Opportunity of Hearing.** Before any **seat reduction or denial**, the college is given a hearing (natural justice) before a **2-member Hearing Committee appointed by the President, MARB**, conducted **virtually (VC)**; a single opportunity — "no further hearing opportunity will be given" on non-attendance (source: Hearing letter without clarification format § body & closing, citing regulation 55(14) MESAR UG Ayurveda; Board meeting Agenda (1) Minutes § Agenda Item No. 6).
9. **Hearing minutes → President → Board.** The committee records per-shortcoming minutes (shortcoming / clarification given / committee observation, with "submission … not considered" verdicts) and places them before the President and the Board (source: Board meeting Agenda (1) Minutes § Agenda Item No. 6 — Shivganga Ayurvedic Medical College AYU0866).
10. **Board decision & letters.** The Board decides permission (full / reduced seats) / conditional (Sowa-Rigpa) / denial; a decision/permission letter is issued, copied to Chairperson NCISM and President MARB (source: PUNITIVE POLICY § all; letter formats § signature/Copy-to blocks).
11. **Penalties / compliance.** Seat reductions, **₹25 lakh per ghost faculty**, teacher-code revocation, denial, and §28(1)(f) action are applied per the punitive policy (source: PUNITIVE POLICY §§ 1–13).
12. **Appeals.** **First appeal → the Commission** (with a hearing); **second appeal → the Central Government / Ministry of Ayush** (source: MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 44(a)–(g)).

## 4. Stage-wise Workflow

Each stage: **trigger → actors → inputs → outputs → decision points**, cited.

**Stage A — Application & scrutiny (Section 29 only).** Trigger: proposal received before the last date (source: MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 42). Actors: College, Dealing staff, Board. Inputs: Forms 29A/C/E, certificates, fees. Outputs: 1st/2nd scrutinization report. Decision point: fulfilling → proceed to visitation; rectifiable → clarification round; **non-rectifiable** (missing essentiality/COA, land dispute, hospital non-functional) → **disapproval without rectification** (source: MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 42(c); Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Item No. 2).

**Stage B — Visitation. ⚠️ Scope: upstream (TCS).** Trigger: annual suo-moto schedule (Sec. 28) or Board direction (Sec. 29) or surprise re-visit. Actors: visitation team. Inputs: college's Part-I/Part-II proformas. Outputs: findings per section 1–8 + evidence (photos, AEBAS extracts, register scans). Decision point: college denies visitation → **denial + all faculty presumed ghost + §28(1)(f)** (source: PUNITIVE POLICY § 12; MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 15).

**Stage C — Regulatory Report & receipt.** The report carries **§8.1 visitor observations** and **§8.2 commission assessment** (a 24-row Yes/No parameter grid), plus certification (source: AYU0265 § 8; AYU0659 § Certification Details). **⚠️ Scope: TCS generates the report; the platform receives it (API — production; Visitor manual upload — interim) and then runs its own extraction → MESAR rule evaluation → punitive computation** to derive the internal MARB assessment (deficiency %s, HF/LF shortfalls, seat-reduction ledger) (source for the derived computation logic: Assessment of Sardar PAtel… § punitive columns; PUNITIVE POLICY §§ 1–13).

**Stage D — Clarification cycle.** Trigger: shortcomings found. Actors: Dealing staff (draft), Board Member (sign), College (respond). Output: clarification letter + logged response. Decision point: all resolved → board-ready for grant; unresolved & adverse → Board with hearing recommendation (source: clarification letter format § all; Hearing letter with clarification format § re-examination).

**Stage E — Board meeting.** Trigger: numbered meeting. Agenda: Item 1 = confirmation of previous minutes, then scrutiny reports, assessment reports, hearing minutes (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Items 1–6). Decision per case: grant / deny / **grant hearing** / defer / re-visit.

**Stage F — Hearing.** Trigger: Board decision to hear before seat-reduction/denial. Actors: President (appoints 2-member committee), Committee (conducts VC), College (submits + attends). Output: per-shortcoming minutes + verdict. Decision point: college absent → forfeits further opportunity, decided on records (source: Hearing letter without clarification format § closing; Board meeting Agenda (1) Minutes § Agenda Item No. 6).

**Stage G — Decision, letters, penalties.** Board decides; decision/permission letter generated with mandatory copy-to; penalties applied (seat reduction, ₹25L ghost-faculty, teacher-code revocation, denial, §28(1)(f)) (source: PUNITIVE POLICY §§ 1–13; letter formats § Copy-to).

**Stage H — Appeals & closure.** First appeal → Commission (with hearing); second appeal → Ministry of Ayush (source: MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 44). The case closes on final order dispatch; compliance/penalty status is monitored to resolution.

## 5. Hearing 1 & Hearing 2 Analysis

**This is the most misunderstood part of the workflow, so it is treated precisely.** The client documents do **not** define two sequential hearings labelled "Hearing 1" and "Hearing 2." What the corpus actually contains is **one hearing stage**, and two _other_ "first/second" constructs that are easily confused with it:

**(a) One Hearing stage, two notice variants.** The corpus has two hearing-letter templates that differ only by whether a **clarification round preceded** the hearing:

- **Hearing _without_ clarification** — the assessment goes straight to a hearing (source: Hearing letter without clarification format — AYU0659, placed in the 124th Board meeting).
- **Hearing _with_ clarification** — a clarification letter was issued, the college replied, the report was re-examined, and _then_ a hearing was granted (source: Hearing letter with clarification format — AYU0221, clarification of 20-06-2025, placed in the 134th Board meeting).

Both feed the **same single Hearing Committee stage**. Both letters state the hearing is a one-time opportunity: _"no further hearing opportunity will be given in case you do not attend"_ (source: Hearing letter without clarification format § closing). **Purpose of the hearing:** natural justice — a mandatory show-cause opportunity **before any seat reduction or denial** (source: both hearing letters § body, citing **regulation 55(14) of MESAR for Undergraduate Ayurveda**). **Trigger:** a Board decision that the case is adverse. **Roles:** President appoints the **2-member** committee; the committee conducts a **virtual (VC)** hearing under the President's supervision. **Inputs:** the assessment report, any prior clarification, and the college's scanned documents submitted by the deadline. **Outputs:** per-shortcoming minutes (shortcoming / clarification / committee observation, with "submission … not considered" verdicts) placed before the President and Board (source: Board meeting Agenda (1) Minutes § Agenda Item No. 6 — AYU0866).

**(b) "First/second" in the regulations = the APPEALS ladder, not hearings.** Where the regulations say "first" and "second," they mean **appeals**: **First appeal → the Commission** (which itself gives an opportunity of hearing), **Second appeal → the Central Government / Ministry of Ayush** (source: MESAR*PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 44(a),(c),(g); mirrored in MESAR_PG_Siddha, MESAR_PG_Unani, MESAR_UG_Unani, MESAR_UG_Sowa-Rigpa appeal clauses). This is a \_post-decision* remedy, distinct from the _pre-decision_ hearing in (a).

**(c) The built system's `hearing1` / `hearing2` are two people, not two hearings.** In the as-built portal, `hearing1@ncism.local` and `hearing2@ncism.local` are the **two committee-member accounts** that make up the single 2-member Hearing Committee (source: demo.md § 3.5 Branch B; NCISM_Assessment_Portal_System_Architecture_Corrected.md § 2 — "Hearing Committee (hearing1 & hearing2)"). They map directly to the **2-member committee** in the corpus (source: Board meeting Agenda (1) Minutes § Agenda Item No. 6 — "the 02 Members of hearing committee undertook the hearing").

**Difference summary:**

|                 | Hearing (pre-decision)                               | First appeal                       | Second appeal                  |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| **Why**         | Natural justice before seat-reduction/denial         | Challenge a denial                 | Challenge the appeal outcome   |
| **Who decides** | 2-member Hearing Committee → Board                   | The Commission                     | Ministry of Ayush              |
| **Trigger**     | Board finds the case adverse                         | Denial issued                      | First appeal rejected          |
| **Source**      | Hearing letters; Agenda (1) Minutes § 6; reg. 55(14) | MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 44(a)–(c) | MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 44(g) |

> **⚠️ Assumption (explicitly flagged).** Any reading of "Hearing 1 vs Hearing 2" as **two sequential hearing rounds** is **not supported by the client documents** — the corpus describes **one** hearing opportunity (forfeited on non-attendance) plus a two-tier **appeals** ladder. Reasoning: the two hearing-letter templates are the _with-/without-clarification_ variants of the same stage; the only "two" in the hearing itself is the **two committee members**; and "first/second" language belongs to the appeals clauses. This inference is drawn from the letter templates + Agenda (1) Minutes + Reg. 44 and cannot be contradicted by any corpus document, but the client should confirm no separate second-hearing practice exists outside the supplied samples.

## 9. Presentation-Ready Demo Flow (chronological)

A clean lifecycle for a single college, in order — every step traceable:

1. **Initiation.** _(Section 29 only)_ College files the proposal + fees; MARB registers it (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § Item 2). _(Section 28 annual cases begin at the visitation.)_
2. **Scrutiny.** Dealing staff build the scrutinization report; a clarification round may run; a 2nd pass follows (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § Item 2).
3. **Inspection / Visitation.** The visitation team visits (physical/virtual/hybrid), verifies staff and facilities, gathers AEBAS/register/photo evidence (source: AYU0659 §§ 1–8). **⚠️ Scope: run by TCS.**
4. **Regulatory Report.** The team produces the report (§8.1 visitor observations, §8.2 commission grid, Certification) (source: AYU0265 § 8). **⚠️ Scope: TCS delivers it to the platform (API; interim upload); the platform then computes deficiencies + the punitive ledger** (source: Assessment of Sardar PAtel… § punitive columns; PUNITIVE POLICY §§ 1–13).
5. **Deficiency handling — clarification.** MARB e-mails the clarification letter; the college responds within the deadline (source: clarification letter format; Hearing letter with clarification format).
6. **Board re-examination.** The report + clarification are tabled at a numbered Board meeting; the Board grants a hearing if the case is adverse (source: Board meeting Agenda (0); hearing letters).
7. **Hearing.** President appoints the 2-member committee; a single VC hearing is held; the college submits documents and attends; per-shortcoming minutes with verdicts are recorded (source: Board meeting Agenda (1) Minutes § 6; hearing letters).
8. **Committee recommendation.** Minutes go to the President and back to the Board (source: Board meeting Agenda (1) Minutes § 6).
9. **Board decision.** Permission (full / reduced) / conditional / denial, with the punitive ledger attached (source: PUNITIVE POLICY §§ 1–13).
10. **Final permission or rejection.** Decision/permission letter issued, copied to Chairperson NCISM & President MARB (source: letter formats § Copy-to). On denial: first appeal → Commission, second → Ministry of Ayush (source: MESAR_PG_Ayurveda § Reg. 44).
11. **Case closure & compliance.** Penalties recorded and monitored to resolution; the case closes (source: PUNITIVE POLICY §§ 4–13).
