## FR-000 series — Authentication & user administration

- **FR-001 · Role-based authenticated access.** All functions require login; access is governed by the role × permission matrix in [file 07](07-roles-permissions.md). Rationale: decisions carry statutory weight (BR-507); segregation between drafting, approving and hearing roles is implied by signature/approval practice (source: Hearing letter without clarification format § "issued with the approval of the competent authority"). Actors: all. Inputs: credentials → Outputs: session with role claims. Depends: NFR security series.
- **FR-002 · User & role administration.** System Admin creates users, assigns roles, activates/deactivates accounts, and maintains the dealing-staff **work-allotment table** (state × system → staff) that drives case routing (BR-103) (source: Work Allotment in Staff.md § Sheet1). Inputs: user details, allotment rows → Outputs: routing configuration, audit entries.
- **FR-003 · College-side accounts.** Each institute has an external account bound to its Institute ID and registered official e-mail (BR-106) for viewing its cases, letters and deadlines and submitting responses (source: UG Ayurveda 2024 § 63(3) — login credentials issued with LOP; § 6 official e-mail) `[INFERRED — whether this reuses the existing NCISM portal login is open: Q-011]`.
- **FR-004 · Delegation & vacancy handling.** Allotment changes (staff transfer/leave) re-route open cases with history preserved. `[INFERRED — from the allotment sheet listing paired staff per state, implying substitution needs]`

## FR-010 series — Institute & master data management

- **FR-011 · Institute registry (CRUD).** Maintain the master of institutes: Institute ID, system of medicine, state, name, structured address, file number, e-mail, contact, plus status attributes (permission category, established/under-establishment, deemed-closed) (source: Master data of institute § All File Number; UG Ayurveda 2024 § 54). Create is restricted (new IDs issued at LOP per BR-102); edits audited. Inputs: master fields → Outputs: registry records, change log.
- **FR-012 · Master data migration & cleansing.** One-time import of the 672-row Excel master with cleansing: split name/address, normalize state names, flag missing e-mail/contact (`-`), fix header spellings (source: Master data of institute § All File Number; CON-007, Q-015). Outputs: migrated registry + exception report.
- **FR-013 · Teacher registry.** Maintain teachers with teacher code, name, designation cadre (Professor/Associate/Assistant), department, institute, state-board registrations, and **code-revocation history** with offence count driving the 1yr/2yr/permanent ladder (BR-104, BR-405) (source: AYU0659 § 3.6; PUNITIVE POLICY § 5). Inputs: staff lists from Part-I, visitation verification → Outputs: canonical teacher records, revocation register.
- **FR-014 · Visitor registry.** Maintain empanelled visitors: Visitor ID, name, home institute, system of medicine, UG/PG eligibility, engagement history (source: AYU0659 § Visitor Details; PG Ayurveda 2024 § Ch. VI 15 — PG-exclusive assessors). Empanelment workflow itself is out of scope pending GAP-009.
- **FR-015 · Regulation & standards tables.** Maintain versioned reference tables per regulation: intake slabs, staffing schedules per department/cadre, area norms, library/equipment requirements, hospital norms (beds, OPD, occupancy), fee matrices, grade bands (source: UG Ayurveda 2024 Schedules I–XXXI & Tables; UG Unani 2023 Schedules I–XXIII; UG Sowa-Rigpa 2023 Schedules I–XIII; PG regs Schedules). Rationale: the assessment engine (FR-040s) evaluates Actual vs Required from these tables; they change only by gazette amendment. Inputs: re-keyed schedule data → Outputs: versioned rule tables (see C-06 re OCR re-keying).
- **FR-016 · Punitive-policy configuration.** Maintain the punitive policy as a versioned rule set effective per academic session, Board-approval reference required (e.g., "160th Board meeting") (BR-401…BR-411) (source: PUNITIVE POLICY § final para). Only Super Admin/Board-Secretariat role may activate a version.
- **FR-017 · Master search.** Search institutes by ID, name, state, system, permission category, status; search teachers by code/name; search visitors by ID/name. Rationale: every letter and agenda item is keyed by these (source: all letters § Subject).

## FR-020 series — Applications & scrutiny (Section 29)

- **FR-021 · Application intake (CRUD).** Register proposals with type (new UG college / new PG programme / standalone PG / intake increase / DM), applicant details, session, received date, addressed-to, and assigned temporary ID for new colleges (BR-102, BR-201) (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Item 2 — "Application dated 28.07.2025, Received on 31.07.2025, Addressed to the President"). Inputs: application data + uploaded documents → Outputs: application case in state `Received`.
- **FR-022 · Regulation-driven document checklist.** On application creation, instantiate the checklist for its type/system/level (Forms 29A–E / A–G, essentiality certificate, consent of affiliation, affidavits, land documents, hospital certificates, registers, salary bank statements, EPF/ESI…) with per-item status: Submitted / Not submitted / Submitted-but-deficient / Not applicable / Submitted-after-clarification, and validity dates for time-bound certificates (fire NOC, pollution control, essentiality valid 2 years) (BR-202) (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Item 2 — full observed checklist incl. "valid up to 2 years", "expired on 23.03.2026").
- **FR-023 · Fee recording & verification.** Record application/processing fees with mode (NEFT/RTGS), UTR/file number, amount, date; flag mismatches against the fee matrix for the type and intake slab (BR-208) (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Item 2 — "NEFT vide file no. 20250711125341 Rs. 14,16,000 (along with GST)"). Reconciliation channel open: Q-012.
- **FR-024 · Scrutiny report generation.** Produce the scrutinization report from checklist state: item, requirement text, submission status, annexure references, clarification history — replacing the hand-built agenda tables (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Item 2). Outputs: versioned scrutiny report (1st, 2nd… pass) attached to the case.
- **FR-025 · Scrutiny clarification cycle.** Issue clarification requests for rectifiable items with deadline; record college responses per item ("After clarification — …"); support multiple passes with the loop bound configurable (AMB-007/Q-002). Non-rectifiable findings (BR-204) route the case to rejection with hearing/appeal rights.
- **FR-026 · Application decision routing.** On scrutiny completion, route to visitation scheduling (FR-030s) and ultimately Board decision; enforce the 6-month decision clock with countdown alerts (BR-206).
- **FR-027 · LOI/LOP/renewal issuance.** Record LOI (29.0), LOP (29.1, triggering permanent Institute ID issuance + portal credentials note), renewals (29.2–29.4), and automatic transition to *fully established* after 3rd renewal (BR-207) (source: UG Ayurveda 2024 §§ 62–64 Table-16). Track security-deposit receipt and 5-year refund eligibility (BR-205; source: UG Ayurveda 2024 § 59).

## FR-030 series — Visitation management — **External (TCS)** + report ingestion

> **⚠️ Scope revision:** visitation execution and Regulatory-Report authoring are performed **externally by TCS** (ASM-002 revision, GAP-011). FR-031–FR-037 below are retained as **domain context** describing the upstream process the client documents evidence — they are **not build scope**. The platform's own requirement in this series is **FR-038 (report ingestion)**.

- **FR-038 · Regulatory-report ingestion.** Receive the complete TCS-generated 20–50-page Regulatory/Assessment Report and register it against the correct institute/session case with provenance (source channel, receipt timestamp, submitter/system identity, content hash — BR-311). **Production channel: the TCS-exposed API (I-11; contract pending — Q-021). Interim channel: authenticated manual upload via the Visitor portal — a temporary workaround until the TCS API is available, not the production architecture.** Validation on receipt: institute resolvable, session stated, file integrity. `⚠️ Assumption — payload shape per ASM-012 (PDF + metadata envelope); no contract exists.` Inputs: report + metadata → Outputs: case in the received state, routed by allotment (BR-103).

- **FR-031 · Visitation creation & team assignment.** Create visitation with Visitation ID, institute, purpose (LOI/annual/renewal/increase/re-visit/surprise), academic session, mode (physical/virtual/hybrid), dates; assign visitor team (default 3 for UG annual — ASM-011) with conflict screening (e.g., visitor's home college ≠ subject college; further rules pending GAP-009) (source: AYU0659 § Visitation Details & Visitor Details; UG Ayurveda 2024 § 55(10) re-visits).
- **FR-032 · Part-I/Part-II intake.** Receive/record the college's Part-I (college) and Part-II (hospital) proforma data for the session, timestamped — the baseline the visitors verify (BR-304) (source: AYU0038 § 8.1; PUNITIVE POLICY § Notes (i)-(ii): assessment basis differs if visit precedes Part-I). Field templates reverse-engineered from filled reports (GAP-002).
- **FR-033 · Digital visitation proforma.** Visitors capture findings against the standard report structure (sections 1–8): admission capacity; constructed areas; common/teaching department areas; library; herbal garden; teaching pharmacy; AEBAS availability; website; teaching staff verification per department/cadre; non-teaching staff; hospital infrastructure & functionality statistics; hospital staff; equipment per department. Every measurable line = Required (auto-filled from FR-015 tables) / Actual / Documents verified / Matches (Y/N) / Visitor observation / Reason for disagreement (source: AYU0659 §§ 1–7 structure). Inputs: on-site entries → Outputs: structured visitation report.
- **FR-034 · Staff verification capture.** Per teacher: present/absent, AEBAS in-time, on attendance register, in acquaintance/salary roll, identity card, eligibility verdict with rejection reason ("Unauthorized absence", "Informed Leave"), teacher-code validity; support the observed counts summary (listed/eligible/present/absent/rejected) (source: AYU0659 §§ 3.2–3.6; Board meeting Agenda (1) Minutes § faculty table with Employee Code/In-time/Status).
- **FR-035 · Evidence attachment.** Attach photos, videos, register scans, AEBAS portal extracts, CCTV references and QR-code photographs to any proforma line or to the visitation; evidence is immutable once the report is submitted (source: Board meeting Agenda (1) Minutes — "QR codes … taken over by the visitor and are attached herewith"; "video evidence is attached").
- **FR-036 · Per-visitor certification & sign-off.** Each visitor answers the certification blocks (teaching staff, non-teaching, hospital staff, hospital functionality, council & website, overall) with Status + Reason-for-disagreement, then signs off; the visitation locks when all visitors certify (source: AYU0659 § Certification Details).
- **FR-037 · Surprise/re-visit support.** Allow unscheduled re-visits linked to the same case; the latest visit's observations supersede for re-assessment (BR-310) (source: PUNITIVE POLICY § Note (i); UG Ayurveda 2024 § 55(10)).

## FR-040 series — Assessment & punitive computation

> These remain **platform scope**: the engine computes from the **received Regulatory Report (FR-038)** — the report's Part-I/II data, proforma sections and visitor observations arrive inside the TCS-generated document.

- **FR-041 · Compliance computation.** From the **received Regulatory Report** (its visitation findings + Part-I/II content), compute per MESAR tables: staff availability % (Available/Required × 100, excluding excess) per teaching/non-teaching/hospital category; HF/LF shortfalls per department (ASM-004); area shortfalls after 20% relaxation (BR-305); library/herbal-garden/equipment compliance with the Essential+General mean (BR-306); hospital functionality metrics (OPD average vs required, bed occupancy %, functional flags) (source: Hearing letter without clarification format § formulas "34/45×100 = 75.55%"; AYU0659 §§ 5.2, 7, 8.2).
- **FR-042 · Punitive-policy engine.** Apply the active session policy (FR-016) to computed deficiencies, producing an itemised **seat-reduction ledger** (rule → deficiency → seats) with additive total, the >50% denial trigger, direct-denial conditions (AEBAS absent, zero-faculty department, visitation denied), monetary penalties and teacher-code revocation proposals (BR-401…BR-412) (source: PUNITIVE POLICY §§ 1–13; Assessment of Sardar PAtel... § 11–13 "Punitive policy" columns). Outputs must be reproducible against corpus worked examples (SC-02).
- **FR-043 · Assessment report generation (MARB computation).** Generate the **internal MARB assessment report** — the platform's computed deficiency/punitive write-up (file no., visit dates, visitor table, sections 1–14 narrative + tables) — from the received Regulatory Report's structured data, eliminating manual "Or"-branch templates (source: Assessment of Sardar PAtel... § all). Distinct from the TCS-generated Regulatory Report, which is the *input* (see glossary). Include the "Other observations" block: pending legal cases, rating eligibility, COA submission, compliance report, visitation-fee status (source: Assessment of Sardar PAtel... § Other Observation).
- **FR-044 · Assessment review & finalization.** Dealing staff draft → reviewer/Member finalizes (authority pending Q-003); finalized reports are immutable and versioned; re-assessment after a re-visit creates a new version linked to the visit (BR-310).
- **FR-045 · AEBAS attendance analysis.** Import/enter AEBAS attendance extracts for a period; compute per-faculty attendance %; flag "suspicious" records per configurable rule (AMB-004/Q-006); attach the flagged list to the assessment (source: Assessment of Sardar PAtel... § 9 — "14 faculties are not meeting the required attendance"; Hearing letter without clarification format § AEBAS — "12 teaching faculty members … suspicious").

## FR-050 series — Clarification cycle

- **FR-051 · Clarification letter generation & dispatch.** Generate the clarification letter from the finalized shortcoming list (ref. no., visit dates/mode, purpose/section, regulation citation, numbered defects, deadline, signatory, copy-to) and dispatch to the institute's official e-mail; log dispatch (BR-501, BR-106) (source: clarification letter format § all). Template validation blocks session/date inconsistencies (CON-002).
- **FR-052 · Clarification response capture.** Record the college's response (letter ref, date, per-shortcoming clarification text, attachments) against each shortcoming; track deadline compliance (source: Hearing letter with clarification format § "you have submitted the clarification vide letter ref. No. SUDACMS/2025-26/177 dated 20-06-2025").
- **FR-053 · Re-examination outcome.** Dealing staff re-examine the report with the clarification and mark each shortcoming resolved/unresolved; unresolved adverse cases route to the Board with a hearing recommendation (BR-502) (source: Hearing letter with clarification format § "assessment report has been carefully examined once again").

## FR-060 series — Board meeting management

- **FR-061 · Agenda assembly.** Create numbered Board meetings (date, time); assemble agenda items from ready cases: minutes confirmation (auto Item 1 from the previous meeting — BR-506), scrutiny reports, assessment reports, hearing minutes, other matters (free-form correspondence items) (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § items 1–6).
- **FR-062 · Decision capture.** Record the Board's decision per agenda item/college: grant permission (full/reduced seats), deny, grant hearing, defer, instruct re-visit; decisions reference the regulation clause and policy items applied (source: Hearing letter without clarification format § "Board decided to provide an opportunity of hearing … as per the regulation 55 (14)").
- **FR-063 · Minutes generation & confirmation.** Generate minutes from captured decisions; circulate; confirm at the next meeting with comment handling (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Item 1 — "No comments have been received … may be considered for confirmation").

## FR-070 series — Hearing management

- **FR-071 · Hearing scheduling & notice.** On a Board hearing decision: appoint committee members (President's approval — BR-503), set date/mode (VC), submission deadline and e-mail; generate the hearing notice in either variant (with prior clarification history / without) with show-cause text naming the at-risk permission (source: Hearing letter with/without clarification formats § all). VC link communicated separately (Q-013).
- **FR-072 · Submission intake.** Record written submissions (scanned PDFs) received by the deadline, tied to specific shortcomings; late/absent submissions flagged; non-attendance triggers the forfeit rule (BR-504).
- **FR-073 · Hearing minutes capture.** During/after the hearing, record per shortcoming: college clarification, committee observation, verdict (considered / not considered with reasons), plus newly discovered findings (e.g., AEBAS proxy-marking evidence) and attendee record (source: Board meeting Agenda (1) Minutes § Agenda Item 6 — three-column minute structure + new AEBAS findings).
- **FR-074 · Hearing outcome routing.** Package minutes as a Board agenda item; on Board decision, proceed to decision issuance (FR-080s); escalations (fraud referrals, one-to-one faculty verification recommendations) tracked as follow-up actions (source: Board meeting Agenda (1) Minutes — "disciplinary and legal action needs to be taken…"; "genuinity … verified by NCISM through one to one physical hearing").

## FR-080 series — Decisions, letters & appeals

- **FR-081 · Decision issuance.** Issue the final decision for the college/session: permission (intake, possibly reduced with the ledger attached), conditional permission (Sowa-Rigpa — BR-301), denial, penalties; effective session adjusted if counselling already notified (BR-411). Outputs: decision record + letter.
- **FR-082 · Letter generation engine.** Centralized templating for all letter types (clarification, hearing notice ×2, permission, denial, seat-reduction, penalty, LOI/LOP/renewal) with: bilingual letterhead & ref/date conventions ("Øekad/Ref. No. … fnukad/Dated"), auto reference numbering (rules pending GAP-008/Q-020), structured merge fields, mandatory copy-to blocks, signatory selection, and validations (dates ordered, session consistent, deadline > issue date — CON-002/003 regression) (source: all letter formats § headers/signature/Copy-to).
- **FR-083 · Dispatch & acknowledgement log.** Log every outbound letter: channel (e-mail), recipient address, timestamp, delivery status; letters visible to the college account (FR-003).
- **FR-084 · Appeal tracking.** Track first appeals (Commission) and second appeals (Ministry of Ayush) as statuses with windows per regulation (BR-508), outcomes, and effect on the case (reconsideration directives) `[INFERRED scope — processing the appeal itself may be out of scope: Q-008]`.
- **FR-085 · Penalty & revocation register.** Register imposed monetary penalties (₹25 lakh ghost faculty; up to ₹1 crore regulation penalties) and teacher-code revocations with durations and offence counts; revocations propagate to the teacher registry (FR-013) and block the teacher in future staff verifications nationwide (BR-405, BR-412) (source: PUNITIVE POLICY §§ 4–5).

## FR-090 series — Documents, notifications, search, reporting

- **FR-091 · Document management.** Central repository for all case documents (applications, certificates, proformas, evidence, letters, minutes) with: type taxonomy from the regulation checklists, per-document metadata (issuer, ref no., issue/valid-until dates), versioning, immutability after finalization, and full-text search. Validity-expiry alerts for time-bound certificates (fire NOC, pollution control, essentiality, COA) (source: Board meeting Agenda (0) § Agenda Item 2 — expired/renewal-pending certificates drive scrutiny outcomes).
- **FR-092 · Notifications & deadline engine.** Configurable notifications (in-app + e-mail): clarification/hearing/submission deadlines, 6-month application clock, 60-day counselling countdown, monthly self-disclosure due (10th), certificate expiries, board-meeting schedules, allotment changes. Escalation to supervisors on breach risk (BR-206, BR-307, BR-302).
- **FR-093 · Global search.** Cross-entity search by Institute ID, college name, state, system, session, file/letter reference, teacher code, visitor ID, case status (source: keys used throughout the corpus).
- **FR-094 · Standard reports.** Generate: state/system-wise pipeline report; session decision summary (granted/reduced/denied counts); punitive-action register; hearing outcomes; deemed-closure watchlist (5-UG/3-PG consecutive tracker — BR-308); fee collection report; visitor engagement report; work-allotment load report (source: report needs implied by Work Allotment sheet totals and Board agenda summaries).
- **FR-095 · Dashboards.** Role-appropriate dashboards: dealing staff (my allotted states — open cases, deadlines), Board/President (cases awaiting decision, hearing calendar, session progress vs counselling date), management (throughput, ageing, penalties issued). See [09-modules.md](09-modules.md).
- **FR-096 · Audit trail.** Every create/update/finalize/approve/dispatch action logged with actor, timestamp, before/after; audit view for authorized roles (supports SC-05; NFR audit series).

## FR-100 series — Rating support (later phase, pending Q-009)

- **FR-102 · Rating data assembly.** Assemble the 70% online component from self-disclosure data and the 30% physical component from visitation records; support Sowa-Rigpa's scored proforma (Annexure-VI/VI-A) and Key-Area scoring for Ayurveda/Unani once parameter sheets are provided (GAP-005) (BR-602).
- **FR-103 · Grade issuance & publication feed.** Compute grade bands (BR-603), record Board approval, and export the publication list for the Commission website before counselling (BR-604).
