**PUNITIVE POLICY ADOPTED TO BE IMPOSED ON THE ASU COLLEGES FOR THE ACADEMIC SESSION 2026-27**

1. **Aadhaar Enabled Biometric Attendance System (AEBAS) (Implementation & functionality): -**
2. The colleges that are found to have not implemented or have partially implemented AEBAS or improperly functional AEBAS, shall be issued **Denial of Permission for the concerned academic year for which the visitation process is underway.**
3. **Minimum Required Criteria & Functionality of the College: -**
4. For each deficient teaching faculty, seat reduction in the intake capacity shall be considered by reducing the **intake strength by 5% of total intake capacity.**
5. For deficiency in **Non-Teaching staff** (excluding Group D staff) seat reduction in the intake capacity shall be considered by reducing the intake strength as under-

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Post** | **Seat Reduction** |
| Librarian | 1 seat |
| Laboratory Technician | 1 seat for 2 deficient staff |

1. A College shall be denied permission for the concerned academic session if any of the teaching departments do not have even a single teaching faculty, during assessment.
2. Ghost faculty/teachers physically absent but present only on paper:- The colleges found maintaining physically absent but present only on paper teachers shall be imposed a monetary penalty of **Rupees Twenty Five lakh per faculty** and the said ghost faculty shall not be considered/counted as a genuine regular faculty in the said medical institution, therefore, only genuine faculty shall be considered for granting permission to the medical institution in respect of seats.
3. The teacher code of the faculty who is found 'Physically absent but present only on-paper' first time**(after NCISM Act 2020 came into force),** shall be **revoked for one year** and the faculty shall be **barred from working as a teacher, Medical Superintendent, Dy. Medical Superintendent or any designation in any medical colleges for the same period.** Further, in case the faculty is found 'Physically absent but present only on-paper' second time, his/her teacher code shall be **revoked for further two years** and the faculty shall be **barred from working as a teacher, Medical Superintendent, Dy. Medical Superintendent or any designation in any medical colleges for the same period.** **Further**, in case the faculty is found 'Physically absent but present only on-paper' third time, his/her teacher code shall be **revoked permanently and he/she** shall be **barred from working as a teacher, Medical Superintendent, Dy. Medical Superintendent or any designation in any medical colleges permanently.**
4. **Minimum Required Criteria & Functionality of the Hospital:-**
5. For deficiency in hospital staff (excluding Group D staff) seat reduction in the intake capacity shall be considered by reducing the intake strength as under:-

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Post** | **Seat Reduction** |
| DMS | 1 seat |
| Matron | 1 seat |
| EMO | 1 seat per deficient staff |
| RMO/RSO/MO | 1 seat per 02 deficient staff |
| Staff Nurse | 1 seat per 03 deficient staff |
| Pharmacist | 1 seat per 02 deficient staff |
| Lab Technician | 1 seat per 02 deficient staff |

1. For non availability of **Computerized Central Registration system** seat reduction in the intake capacity shall be considered by reducing the intake strength by one seat.
2. For every 10% deficiency in **Average attendance of patients in OPD per day** seat reduction in the intake capacity shall be considered by reducing the intake strength by one seat.
3. For every 1% deficiency in **Bed occupancy**, seat reduction in the intake capacity shall be considered by reducing the intake strength by one seat.
4. In case of deficiency in Functionality of Hospital, seat reduction in the intake capacity shall be considered by reducing the intake strength by 30%.

|  |
| --- |
| **Functionality of Hospital mentioned in the above point includes:-**   * Required number of functional OPDs & IPDs * Functionality of Clinical Laboratory, Operation Theatre, Labour Room, Panchakarma block, Ksharsutra block, and the overall functionality of the IPD & OPD. |

* **In case of multiple deficiencies as specified above, all the deficiencies will be added and intake strength shall be reduced accordingly.**
* **In case the calculated seat reduction is more than 50%, after considering all the deficiencies mentioned above, under the functionality of college and hospital, then the college shall be denied permission for that particular academic session.**

1. **Miscellaneous:-**
2. **Denial of visitation by the college**

The colleges denying the official visitation scheduled by the Board shall be issued denial of permission for the concerned academic year for which the said visitation is scheduled and all faculty shall be presumed as 'Physically absent but present only on paper'. All the punitive actions against on-paper teachers shall be applicable to such medical institutions and they shall be liable to face action under section 28(1) (f) of NCISM Act and concerned regulations therein.

13. **Non-compliance to the instructions issued by the Board/Commission**

The failure in timely compliance of instructions of the Commission/Board without valid justification will attract strict disciplinary action against the Head of the Institution as well as the college, as per **section 28(1) (f) of NCISM Act 2020.**

**NOTE:-**

**Note (i):-**

The colleges which have been inspected by the Board through inspection after the issuance of permission/denial for the concerned Academic Year after their due annual inspection and assessment, shall be re-assessed on the basis of observations of the latter i.e. the latest visit, along with the Part-I visitation proforma submitted by the college and other necessary documents as and when demanded from the college by MARB-ISM.

**Note (ii):-**

The colleges visited before submission of Part One visitation proforma/ before the annual visitation, shall assessed on the basis of the observations of the said visit itself for the purpose of issuing permission/ denial for the concerned academic session. However, the Board reserves the right to decide if another visitation(s) is/are necessary as per regulation 55 (10) of MESA&R UG Ayurveda regulations 2024, regulation 60 (3)(k) of MESA&R UG Siddha regulations 2024 and regulation 16 (3)(k) of MESA&R UG Unani regulations 2023.

**Note (iii):-**

For a college conducting both UG and PG courses, in case, the UG course is denied for a particular academic session, then simultaneously the corresponding PG courses conducted by the college for the same academic session shall also deemed to be denied.

**Note (iv):-**

If the college is found compliant with the NCISM Act, 2020, the concerned regulations and the applicable relaxation policy, if any, for the concerned Academic Year, eligible permission may be granted. However, in case where the college is found non-compliant with the provisions of the NCISM Act, 2020 and the relevant regulations, this punitive policy along with concerned regulations shall be applicable.

**Note (v):-**

In case the college had already been issued Permission for the particular academic year and counselling process has been notified, the punitive action as mentioned under section 28(1) (f) of NCISM Act and concerned regulations shall be applicable for the ensuing/subsequent academic year.

**The above derived punitive policy is approved by the Medical Assessment and Rating Board for Indian System of Medicine in its 160th Board meeting and the same shall be applicable for the academic session 2026-27.**
